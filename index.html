About Parkinson's disease

Parkinson's disease, a complex neurological condition, impairs movement and affects millions worldwide. This condition arises from the progressive loss of dopamine-producing cells in the brain, particularly in the substantia nigra.

As Parkinson's progresses, individuals may experience debilitating symptoms, including tremors, muscle stiffness, slowed movements, and challenges with balance and coordination. These symptoms not only impact physical abilities but also significantly affect daily life and emotional well-being.
